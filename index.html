Frendly

android • ui design • mobile computing • sustainable design

introduction

Frendly is an android mobile application aimed to help families and households to sell, rent or buy second hand items. It is inspired by social media apps, such as Instagram, in order to help users to connect, chat, follow each other and find items that they are interested in.

background

problem statement

Families with children need to buy an awful lot of stuff such as, clothes, toys, pets, books etc. This is very expensive and some of the things are perhaps only used for a shorter period of time. A smarter and more sustainable solution could be if you could simply rent what you need for a period of time and also let others rent things you have but don't really need currently. The assignment for this proeject was to create an app for this market following the Android Material Guidelines.

goals

How can I design an application which can help families to efficiently save money by selling and buying second hand items?

ideation

brainstorming

I created Frendly, a hybrid between a shopping platform and social media application, where users create profiles in order to connect with other Frendly users. Inside the platform, users can sell, rent and buy items, or chat, connect, and follow other users to stay up to date with their activity on the app. Frendly is heavily inspired by social media apps, in order to make it more friendly and to help users connect with each other, but also to increase familiarity and decrease the learning curve. The applications that were mostly used for inspiration for this project were Plick (second hand shopping app), Instagram (social media app), and Blocket (second hand shopping platform). The UI design of Frendly is based on the Andriod Material Guidelines.

what

A mobile application for second hand shopping for families.

why

Help families to save money and shop in a sustainable way.

who

Primarily families with children.

when & where

When families are in need of new things that can be expensive, or when families are not longer in need of items and would like to sell them.

prototype

style guide

The UI design is based on the Android Material Guidelines. I wanted the style and the navigation of the application to feel modern and minimalistic for simplicity and readibility, yet playful and deep for frendliness and brand strengthening. I therefore decided to make the navigation, terminology and icons simple and clean. I decided to challenge myself by designing a dark mode style as default, as dark mode has been proven to reduce eye strain and increase readability.
